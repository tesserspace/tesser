## Market Data (The Inputs)

These concepts map directly to types in `tesser-core` and `tesser-data`.

### Order Book (L2 Data)
A real-time list of buy and sell orders organized by price level.
*   **Bids**: Buyers wanting to buy low.
*   **Asks**: Sellers wanting to sell high.
*   **Spread**: The gap between the highest Bid and the lowest Ask.
*   **Tesser Type**: `OrderBook`, `OrderBookLevel`.

### Tick
An individual trade that happened on the exchange. It tells you the "truth" of what price was actually transacted.
*   **Tesser Type**: `Tick` (contains price, size, side, timestamp).

### OHLCV (Candles/Klines)
Aggregated data. Instead of streaming millions of ticks, we group them into time buckets (e.g., 1 minute).
*   **Open/High/Low/Close**: The price movement within that time window.
*   **Volume**: How much asset was traded.
*   **Tesser Type**: `Candle`.

## Execution (The Actions)

These concepts map to `tesser-execution` and `tesser-broker`.

### Maker vs. Taker
*   **Maker (Passive)**: You place a Limit Order that doesn't fill immediately. It sits in the order book, providing liquidity. usually pays lower fees (or gets a rebate).
*   **Taker (Aggressive)**: You place an order (Market or Limit) that fills immediately against an existing order. You "take" liquidity and pay higher fees.
*   **Tesser Logic**: If you use `OrderType::Limit` inside the spread, you are a Maker. If you use `OrderType::Market`, you are a Taker.

### Slippage
The difference between the price you *expected* to get and the price you *actually* got.
*   **Cause**: In the milliseconds it takes your order to reach the exchange, prices moved; or your order was too big for the available liquidity at the top of the book.
*   **Tesser Context**: Configured in `BacktestConfig` via `slippage_bps` to simulate this cost realistically.

### Time in Force (TIF)
Instructions on how long an order should remain active.
*   **GTC (Good Til Canceled)**: Stays open until filled or you cancel it. (Default).
*   **IOC (Immediate Or Cancel)**: Fill what you can immediately, cancel the rest.
*   **FOK (Fill Or Kill)**: Fill the entire order immediately or cancel the whole thing.
*   **Tesser Type**: `TimeInForce` enum.

## Portfolio & Risk (The State)

These concepts map to `tesser-portfolio`.

### Long vs. Short
*   **Long**: You buy expecting the price to go up. `Position.quantity` is positive.
*   **Short**: You sell (borrowed asset) expecting price to go down. `Position.quantity` is negative (in Tesser's internal accounting).
*   **Tesser Type**: `Side::Buy` (Long entry/Short exit) vs `Side::Sell` (Short entry/Long exit).

### PnL (Profit and Loss)
*   **Unrealized PnL**: "Paper profits". The value of your open position if you were to close it right now at the market price.
*   **Realized PnL**: Actual money made or lost after closing a position.
*   **Tesser Context**: The `Portfolio` struct tracks `unrealized_pnl` on every tick update.

### Drawdown
The percentage drop from the highest point (Peak Equity) your portfolio has ever reached.
*   **Why it matters**: Deep drawdowns are hard to recover from (a 50% loss requires a 100% gain to break even).
*   **Tesser Context**: `risk_max_drawdown` in config triggers "Liquidate Only" mode to stop a bleeding strategy.

## Derivatives Specifics

Since Tesser defaults to Bybit Linear Perpetuals (Crypto Futures), these are crucial:

### Perpetual Contract
A future contract that never expires. To keep the price tethered to the spot price, exchanges use a **Funding Rate**.
*   **Positive Funding**: Longs pay Shorts.
*   **Negative Funding**: Shorts pay Longs.

### Margin
The collateral (usually USDT) you put up to open a position. 
*   **Leverage**: Using borrowed money. If you have 100 USDT and open a 1000 USDT position, you are at 10x leverage.
*   **Liquidation**: If your margin isn't enough to cover Unrealized PnL losses, the exchange forcibly closes your position and takes your collateral.
